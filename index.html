Report · Munich Security Conference · 2023

Fog of War

Translated cybersecurity intelligence research into a printed report distributed during bilateral meetings at the Munich Security Conference — designed for heads of state and key opinion formers during an active war.

Heads of state  ·  Key opinion formers  ·  Policy leaders

+

Year

2023

Format

Print report · Digital assets · Blog

Role

Creative lead · Design direction

Context

Munich Security Conference

The problem

Google's Threat Intelligence team had produced rigorous research on the role of cybersecurity in the Russia-Ukraine war. The challenge was translating that research — written by and for security analysts — into something that heads of state and senior policymakers could act on during one of the most consequential security conferences of the year.

The approach

Working closely with security threat analysts to ensure technical accuracy, I directed the design of a printed report built for bilateral meeting rooms — not conference halls. Every visual decision was made in service of comprehension at the highest level of government. The report was simultaneously launched on The Keyword blog with accompanying digital assets.

Film · Executive Produced & Directed · 2024

AI Safety Film

Conceived and directed a short film about strengthening digital security with AI — produced for the Munich Security Conference and later deployed as a cornerstone asset for the Google AI Cyber Defense Initiative launch.

Global security community  ·  Policy makers  ·  ai.google

+

Year

2024

Format

Short film

Role

Executive producer · Director

Deployed

Munich Security Conference · ai.google

The problem

Google needed to communicate a nuanced argument about AI's role in strengthening — not threatening — global cybersecurity, to an audience of senior policymakers and security professionals who were actively skeptical of AI. A deck or report wouldn't land. The idea required a different medium.

The approach

I conceived and executive produced a short film, working with an agency to bring it to life under my creative direction. The film became the centrepiece of Google's presence at the Munich Security Conference and was later deployed as a primary asset when Google launched the AI Cyber Defense Initiative.

Initiative Launch · Creative Direction

AI Cyber Defense Initiative

Creative direction for Google's AI Cyber Defense Initiative — a major policy and product launch positioning Google's AI capabilities as a force for global cybersecurity, from film to digital assets to launch communications.

Global policy community  ·  Security professionals  ·  Public

+

Year

2024

Format

Film · Digital assets · Launch comms

Role

Creative direction

Platform

blog.google · ai.google

The problem

Google was launching a major initiative to position AI as a tool for strengthening — not undermining — global cybersecurity. The launch needed to work simultaneously for the global policy community, security professionals, and a broader public audience without losing credibility in any of those rooms.

The approach

I provided creative direction across the full suite of launch assets — from the AI Safety Film that anchored the initiative's narrative, to the digital assets and communications that supported the launch on blog.google and ai.google. The film created at the Munich Security Conference became the emotional and argumentative centre of the initiative.
